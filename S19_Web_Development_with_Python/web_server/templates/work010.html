Project Overview

This project was conducted for Taste of the World Cafe to evaluate customer response to their new menu launch. The project involved comprehensive analysis of a quarter's worth of order data to identify popular menu items, understand customer preferences, and provide data-driven recommendations for menu optimization.

The analysis helped the restaurant management make informed decisions about which dishes to promote, which categories to expand, and how to better align their offerings with customer preferences.

Business Context

Taste of the World Cafe introduced a new diverse menu at the beginning of the year and needed to understand how customers were responding to the changes. The restaurant management sought insights into which items were performing well, customer spending patterns, and opportunities for menu improvement.

Primary Question: How can the analysis of restaurant order data reveal customer preferences, identify popular menu items, and guide strategic menu optimization decisions?

Methodology & Analysis

1 Menu Items Exploration

  • Analyzed the complete menu structure and pricing across different categories
  • Identified price ranges and distribution within each cuisine type
  • Examined Italian dishes specifically for pricing and variety
  • Calculated average prices per category to understand pricing strategy
-- Analyzing menu item distribution by category
SELECT category, COUNT(*) as item_count,
ROUND(AVG(price), 2) as avg_price
FROM menu_items
GROUP BY category
ORDER BY item_count DESC;

2 Order Details Analysis

  • Determined the date range and volume of orders in the dataset
  • Analyzed order size patterns and identified large orders
  • Examined distribution of items per order to understand customer behavior
  • Identified orders with unusually high item counts for further investigation

3 Customer Behavior Analysis

  • Joined menu_items and order_details tables for comprehensive analysis
  • Identified most and least popular items by order frequency
  • Analyzed highest spending orders and their composition
  • Examined category performance across different customer segments
  • Provided recommendations for menu optimization
-- Top 5 highest spending orders analysis
SELECT od.order_id, SUM(mi.price) as total_spent,
COUNT(od.item_id) as items_ordered
FROM order_details od
JOIN menu_items mi ON od.item_id = mi.menu_item_id
GROUP BY od.order_id
ORDER BY total_spent DESC
LIMIT 5;

Key Findings

Menu Performance Insights

Customer Behavior Insights

  • Most Popular Item: Hamburger (American category) was ordered 622 times
  • Least Popular Item: Edamame (Asian category) was ordered only 123 times
  • Highest Spending Order: $192.15 on a mixed-category order
  • Average Order Size: 2.3 items per order across all customers
  • Peak Order Times: Lunch (12-2 PM) and Dinner (6-8 PM) hours showed highest activity
Critical Insight: While Middle Eastern dishes had the highest average price point, they showed the lowest order frequency, indicating either a pricing issue or lack of customer familiarity with these dishes.

Business Impact

The analysis provided Taste of the World Cafe with actionable insights to:

  • Optimize menu offerings by focusing on popular, high-margin items
  • Identify underperforming dishes for potential removal or improvement
  • Adjust staffing levels based on order volume patterns
  • Create targeted promotions for less popular but profitable items
  • Develop future menu based on cuisine popularity
  • Price menu items more effectively based on value perception
Recommendation: Develop combo meals featuring popular American items paired with less-familiar Middle Eastern dishes to increase exposure and trial of underperforming categories while leveraging the popularity of established favorites.
