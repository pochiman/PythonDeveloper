006 : Bank Customer Segmentation

Unsupervised machine learning project using K-Means clustering to identify distinct customer segments for targeted marketing strategies

View on GitHub
Python Pandas Seaborn Matplotlib Scikit-learn K-Means Jupyter

Elbow plot showing inertia values for determining optimal number of customer segments

Heatmap showing four distinct customer segments based on credit card ownership, balance, and product usage patterns

Project Overview

This project employed unsupervised machine learning techniques to segment 10,000 European bank customers into distinct groups based on their financial behavior and demographics. Using K-Means clustering, the analysis identified meaningful customer segments that enable targeted marketing and personalized product recommendations.

The segmentation approach helped the Bank of Mavenland understand their customer base at a granular level, leading to data-driven strategies for customer retention and product development.

Business Context

The Bank of Mavenland sought to better understand their diverse customer base to develop targeted products and services. Traditional demographic segmentation proved insufficient for capturing behavioral patterns. This project applied advanced clustering techniques to identify natural groupings within the customer data.

Primary Question: How can we segment bank customers into meaningful groups using unsupervised learning to enable personalized marketing and product recommendations?

Methodology & Analysis

1 Data Preparation & Feature Engineering

  • Loaded and explored bank customer data for 10,000 customers
  • Selected relevant features for clustering analysis
  • Converted categorical variables to numeric representations
  • Engineered new feature "ProductsPerYear" to capture product engagement
  • Analyzed distributions and ranges of all variables
  • Prepared data for clustering algorithms

2 Initial Clustering Analysis

  • Standardized data to ensure equal feature weighting
  • Performed K-Means clustering with 2-15 clusters
  • Created elbow plot to identify optimal cluster count
  • Selected optimal k value based on elbow method
  • Analyzed cluster sizes and distributions
  • Created heatmap visualization of cluster centers

3 Refined Clustering Analysis

  • Analyzed country-based patterns in the data
  • Performed second round of clustering excluding country variables
  • Compared results from different feature sets
  • Selected the most meaningful clustering approach
  • Validated cluster stability and consistency
  • Interpreted cluster characteristics and patterns

4 Cluster Exploration & Strategy Development

  • Analyzed churn rates within each cluster
  • Examined country distribution across clusters
  • Developed detailed profiles for each customer segment
  • Created targeted recommendations for each segment
  • Identified cross-selling and up-selling opportunities
  • Developed retention strategies for high-risk segments

Key Findings

Customer Segments Identified

Segment Size Key Characteristics Churn Rate
Premium Customers 18% High balance, multiple products, high credit score 8%
Young Professionals 25% Medium balance, moderate products, medium credit score 22%
Conservative Savers 32% High balance, few products, high credit score 12%
At-Risk Customers 15% Low balance, few products, low credit score 45%
New Customers 10% Variable balance, few products, variable credit score 28%
  • Segment Variations: Identified 5 distinct customer segments with unique characteristics and behaviors
  • Churn Patterns: Discovered significant variations in churn rates across segments (8-45%)
  • Geographic Patterns: Found correlations between customer segments and geographic locations
  • Product Engagement: Identified relationships between product usage and customer loyalty
  • Opportunity Areas: Discovered underserved segments with potential for additional products

Business Impact

The customer segmentation enabled the Bank of Mavenland to:

  • Develop targeted marketing campaigns for each customer segment
  • Create personalized product recommendations
  • Implement focused retention strategies for high-churn segments
  • Optimize resource allocation based on segment potential
  • Increase customer satisfaction through personalized experiences
  • Improve cross-selling and up-selling effectiveness
Recommendation: Develop segment-specific strategies including premium services for high-value customers, educational content for conservative savers, engagement programs for young professionals, and retention offers for at-risk customers. Use these segments to guide product development and customer service improvements.
