Project Overview

This project involved analyzing Morocco's electricity consumption data measured at 10-minute intervals to identify seasonal patterns, peak load periods, and correlations with environmental factors. The analysis helped inform infrastructure investment decisions to prevent outages during high-demand periods.

The project demonstrates my expertise in time series analysis, data visualization, and deriving actionable insights from large-scale temporal data to support critical infrastructure planning.

Business Context

Morocco's National Power Company needed to assess plans for upgrading its infrastructure by understanding electricity consumption patterns. This analysis focused on identifying peak load periods across different timescales (hourly, daily, weekly, and seasonal) to optimize investments and prevent service disruptions.

Primary Question: When do peak electricity consumption periods occur, and what environmental factors correlate most strongly with usage patterns?

Methodology & Analysis

1 Data Preparation & Feature Engineering

  • Processed electricity consumption data measured at 10-minute intervals across 3 zones
  • Engineered features by extracting date parts (hour, day of week, month) from datetime stamps
  • Created calculated columns for total consumption across all zones
  • Resampled high-frequency data into appropriate time intervals for analysis
  • Integrated environmental data including temperature, humidity, and wind speed

2 Time Series Visualization

  • Created stacked line charts to visualize consumption patterns for January 2017
  • Analyzed consumption trends across different time intervals (hourly, daily, weekly)
  • Identified peak consumption periods and seasonal patterns
  • Compared consumption patterns across different zones
  • Correlated consumption data with environmental factors

3 Seasonality Analysis with Heatmaps

  • Created pivot tables to aggregate consumption by day of week and hour of day
  • Developed heatmap visualizations to identify consumption patterns
  • Implemented day name mapping instead of integer representations
  • Analyzed consumption differences between weekdays and weekends
  • Identified the strongest correlating environmental factors with electricity usage

Key Findings

  • Peak Consumption Periods: Identified consistent daily peaks between 6-9 PM and seasonal peaks during summer months
  • Temperature Correlation: Discovered the strongest relationship between temperature and electricity consumption (r=0.82)
  • Weekly Patterns: Found significantly different consumption patterns on weekends compared to weekdays
  • Zone Variations: Identified distinct consumption patterns across the three measured zones
  • Infrastructure Implications: Provided data-driven recommendations for targeted infrastructure investments

Business Impact

The analysis provided Morocco's National Power Company with data-driven insights into:

  • Specific time periods requiring infrastructure reinforcement
  • Expected peak demand periods for load balancing
  • Correlation between environmental factors and electricity consumption
  • Regional variations in consumption patterns
  • Data-informed investment prioritization for grid upgrades
Recommendation: Focus infrastructure investments on capacity improvements targeting 6-9 PM peak periods and prepare for increased summer demand, particularly during heat waves when temperature correlates strongly with consumption spikes.
