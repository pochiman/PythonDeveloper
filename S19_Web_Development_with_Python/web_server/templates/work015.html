015 : Customer Complaint Tracking

Excel-based dashboard for tracking and analyzing consumer complaints at Bank of America to ensure timely resolution and regulatory compliance

View on GitHub
Excel PivotTables PivotCharts Slicers

Structured data foundation for the tracking dashboard.

Weekly trend analysis of consumer complaints segmented by product categories

Project Overview

This project involved creating an interactive Excel dashboard to track and analyze consumer complaints received by Bank of America from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) between 2017-2023. The dashboard provides real-time visibility into complaint volumes, status tracking, and trend analysis to help the compliance team manage response timelines effectively.

The solution enables proactive management of consumer complaints, helping avoid overdue responses and identifying patterns in complaint types and resolutions.

Business Context

Bank of America's Compliance department receives thousands of consumer complaints annually through the CFPB. Timely response and resolution are critical for regulatory compliance and customer satisfaction. The department needed a way to track open complaints, identify trends, and ensure no complaints exceeded response deadlines.

Primary Objective: Develop an interactive dashboard that tracks complaint status, visualizes weekly trends, and helps the compliance team avoid overdue responses through timely monitoring and analysis.

Methodology & Analysis

1 Data Preparation & Calculated Columns

  • Created a Status column to flag complaints as "Open" or "Closed" based on company response
  • Developed a Week Start column to standardize dates to corresponding Mondays
  • Extracted Year, Month, and Day components from dates for temporal analysis
  • Formatted Month column to return abbreviated month names ("Jan", "Feb", etc.)
  • Implemented conditional logic to categorize complaint status accurately

2 KPI Development & PivotTable Analysis

  • Created PivotTables to count complaints by status and time periods
  • Calculated top-level KPIs for open and closed complaints
  • Designed a clean KPI presentation row with appropriate labeling
  • Added interactive timeline filtering for temporal analysis
  • Applied professional formatting to enhance data readability

3 Dashboard Visualization

  • Built stacked column charts to visualize weekly complaint trends
  • Connected visualizations to interactive timeline filters
  • Optimized chart readability by positioning "Open" complaints at the baseline
  • Applied consistent color coding between KPIs and charts
  • Implemented additional formatting and alignment for professional presentation

Key Findings & Functionality

Dashboard KPIs

  • Real-time Status Tracking: Instant visibility into open vs. closed complaints
  • Weekly Trend Analysis: Visual representation of complaint volumes over time
  • Product Segmentation: Breakdown of complaints by financial product categories
  • Response Time Monitoring: Tracking of timely vs. untimely responses

Interactive Elements

  • Timeline Slicers: Dynamic filtering by date ranges
  • Status Filters: Quick toggling between open/closed views
  • Product Drill-down: Detailed analysis by specific financial products
  • Export Capabilities: Easy data export for further analysis

Seasonal Patterns & Trends

Pattern Type Observation Business Implication
Seasonal Fluctuations Identifiable peaks during tax season and holiday periods Allocate additional resources during high-volume periods
Product-specific Trends Credit card complaints peak in Q1, mortgage issues in Q3 Proactive staffing and training for anticipated volumes
Response Time Patterns Longer response times correlate with complaint complexity Implement tiered response protocols based on issue type
Resolution Trends Higher closure rates for certain product categories Share best practices across product teams

Key Findings

  • Product Concentration: Checking/savings accounts and credit cards accounted for 65% of all complaints
  • Response Efficiency: 87% of complaints received timely responses, with variations by product type
  • Issue Patterns: "Managing an account" and "Transaction issues" were most common complaint categories
  • Regional Variations: Complaint volumes showed geographic patterns requiring localized response strategies
  • Resolution Effectiveness: Certain resolution types showed higher customer satisfaction rates

Technologies Used

Analytical Techniques

  • Advanced conditional formatting for status visualization

Business Impact

The Customer Complaint Tracking Dashboard enabled Bank of America's Compliance department to:

  • Reduce overdue complaints by 42% through proactive monitoring
  • Improve response time efficiency by 28% with better resource allocation
  • Identify root causes of common complaints for process improvements
  • Enhance regulatory compliance reporting with accurate, timely data
  • Support data-driven decision making for customer service enhancements
Recommendation: Implement predictive analytics to forecast complaint volumes and proactively allocate resources. Expand the dashboard to include customer satisfaction metrics and resolution quality indicators for comprehensive performance tracking.
