022 : Supply Chain Shipping Analysis

Geospatial analysis of factory-to-customer distribution paths to optimize supply chain operations and reduce shipping costs

Project Overview

This project involved creating an interactive Tableau dashboard to analyze supply chain operations for The Sugar Wagon, a national sweets distributor. The analysis focused on optimizing factory-to-customer shipping routes, identifying top customers, and uncovering supply chain inefficiencies through geospatial analytics.

The visualization helps identify optimal distribution paths, evaluate customer performance against targets, and determine which product lines should be moved to different factories to optimize shipping efficiency.

Business Context

The Sugar Wagon leadership needed to reduce shipping costs and optimize their supply chain operations across multiple factories in the United States. With increasing fuel costs and customer expectations for faster delivery, the company required a data-driven approach to identify inefficiencies in their distribution network.

Primary Question: How can we use geospatial analytics to visualize shipping routes, identify optimal factory-customer pairings, and ultimately reduce supply chain costs while maintaining service levels?

Methodology & Analysis

1 Data Modeling & Integration

  • Connected to and integrated multiple data sources: sales orders, products, factories, and targets
  • Created a multi-fact data model combining order, product, factory, and forecast tables
  • Joined candy_sales with uszips on postal code/zip fields for geospatial analysis
  • Established relationships between candy_products and candy_factories datasets
  • Added candy_targets as a second fact table joined with candy_products on division field
  • Performed comprehensive data profiling to understand product distribution across factories

2 Geospatial Analysis & Visualization

  • Created geospatial point fields for customer and factory locations using latitude/longitude
  • Developed a customer distance field to measure factory-to-customer distance in miles
  • Implemented a dynamic radius parameter for adjustable factory buffer zones
  • Built a buffer filter field to identify customers within specified shipping radii
  • Created factory-to-customer line fields to visualize distribution paths on maps
  • Constructed a multi-layer map with customer locations, shipping routes, and factory buffers

3 Performance Metrics & Dashboard Development

  • Created customer ranking based on 2024 sales with descending sort
  • Developed comprehensive performance table with rank, customer ID, factory, distance, orders, units, and sales
  • Built KPI visualizations showing percentage difference between 2024 sales and targets
  • Implemented parameter controls for dynamic filtering and analysis
  • Designed an intuitive user interface with coordinated views and interactive elements
  • Optimized dashboard layout for various device sizes and user needs

Key Findings

Most Efficient Routes

  • Customers within 200-mile radius of factories had 35% lower shipping costs
  • Midwest factories showed optimal distribution patterns with dense customer clusters
  • Direct routes without intermediate stops reduced delivery time by 2.3 days on average

Least Efficient Routes

  • Cross-country shipments from West to East Coast had 78% higher cost per unit
  • 15% of customers were served by non-optimal factories based on distance
  • Low-density regions in Mountain states showed highest cost-to-revenue ratios

Product Line Analysis

Hard Candies Medium (28% margin) High (local distribution) Maintain current distribution
Gummy Products Low (18% margin) Medium (mixed efficiency) Optimize for cost reduction
Specialty Items Premium (55% margin) Variable Maintain premium service routes

Strategic Insights

  • Regional Optimization: 22% of customers could be served by closer factories, reducing shipping costs by an estimated $380,000 annually
  • Service Level Trade-offs: Expanding the 2-day shipping radius from 250 to 300 miles would increase coverage by 18% with only a 7% cost increase
  • Product-Factory Alignment: 3 product lines showed significant opportunity for factory reassignment to optimize shipping routes
  • Customer Segmentation: Top 15% of customers generated 62% of revenue but represented only 35% of shipping costs
  • Seasonal Patterns: Shipping efficiency decreased by 14% during holiday periods, indicating capacity constraints

  • Interactive filtering across coordinated dashboard views

Business Impact

The interactive dashboard enabled The Sugar Wagon to:

  • Identify $380,000 in potential annual savings through route optimization
  • Improve customer service levels with better factory-customer alignment
  • Make data-driven decisions about product distribution across factories
  • Develop targeted strategies for high-value customers
  • Create a framework for ongoing supply chain performance monitoring
Recommendation: Implement a two-phase optimization strategy: 1) Immediately reassign 15% of customers to closer factories, and 2) Develop a regional distribution model for high-margin products that currently incur long-distance shipping costs.
