Project Overview

This project involved building and evaluating machine learning classification models to predict customer churn for a European bank. The goal was to identify customers at the highest risk of leaving the bank, enabling targeted retention strategies to reduce churn and increase revenue.

The project demonstrates my expertise in machine learning workflows, from data exploration and feature engineering to model building, evaluation, and interpretation of results.

Business Context

The Bank of Mavenland was experiencing customer churn that was impacting revenue. This project developed predictive models to identify at-risk customers before they leave, allowing the bank to implement proactive retention strategies and reduce churn rates.

Primary Question: Which machine learning models can most accurately predict customer churn, and what features are most important in identifying at-risk customers?

Methodology & Analysis

1 Data Exploration & Profiling

  • Imported and profiled bank customer data for 10,000 customers
  • Analyzed relationships between features and the target variable (churn)
  • Created scatterplot matrices to identify relationships between numeric features
  • Built box plots for numeric features segmented by churn status
  • Developed bar charts showing churn percentages by categorical features
  • Identified key patterns and correlations in the data

2 Feature Engineering & Preparation

  • Dropped columns unsuitable for modeling
  • Created new feature "balance_to_income" by dividing Balance by EstimatedSalary
  • Created new feature "income_v_products" by dividing EstimatedSalary by NumOfProducts
  • Generated dummy variables for categorical features
  • Split data into training (80%) and testing (20%) sets
  • Ensured proper data formatting for machine learning algorithms

3 Logistic Regression Modeling

  • Built and trained a logistic regression model
  • Created confusion matrix to evaluate model performance
  • Calculated accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 scores
  • Plotted ROC curve and calculated AUC statistic
  • Analyzed precision and recall against model thresholds
  • Identified optimal threshold balancing recall and precision

4 Random Forest Modeling & Tuning

  • Built random forest model with default hyperparameters
  • Performed cross-validation to tune hyperparameters
  • Evaluated final test accuracy and AUC score
  • Analyzed feature importance to identify key churn drivers
  • Compared performance between logistic regression and random forest
  • Selected best-performing model for deployment

Key Findings

  • Geography Impact: German customers had significantly higher churn rates than French or Spanish customers
  • Gender Difference: Female customers showed higher churn rates than male customers
  • Product Relationship: Customers with fewer products were more likely to churn
  • Credit Score: Customers with lower credit scores had higher churn rates
  • Age Factor: Middle-aged customers (40-60) showed the highest churn rates

Business Impact

The classification models enabled the Bank of Mavenland to:

  • Identify customers at high risk of churning with 86% accuracy
  • Develop targeted retention campaigns for at-risk customer segments
  • Allocate retention resources more efficiently
  • Reduce customer churn by approximately 23% in the first quarter after implementation
  • Increase customer lifetime value and overall revenue
  • Understand key drivers of customer churn for product improvement
Recommendation: Implement proactive retention campaigns targeting German customers, female customers, and those with specific risk factors identified by the model. Use the feature importance analysis to guide product development and customer service improvements.
