017 : HR Survey Analysis

Comprehensive analysis of employee engagement survey data for the Seattle Department of Public Works to measure job satisfaction and identify opportunities for improvement

Project Overview

This project involved analyzing employee engagement survey data from approximately 1,500 public works employees for the Seattle Department of Public Works. The analysis aimed to measure job satisfaction, identify key drivers of engagement, and provide data-driven recommendations to HR leadership for improving employee motivation and retention.

The project demonstrates advanced Excel skills including data profiling, cleaning, visualization, and the creation of professional dashboards for stakeholder presentation.

Business Context

The Seattle Department of Public Works conducted a voluntary employee engagement survey to gauge job satisfaction across its workforce. HR leadership needed to understand employee sentiments, identify areas of concern, and develop targeted strategies to improve workplace satisfaction and retention rates.

Primary Question: What are the key factors influencing employee satisfaction at the Seattle Department of Public Works, and what specific actions can leadership take to improve engagement and retention?

Methodology & Analysis

1 Data Profiling & Quality Assessment

  • Calculated minimum, maximum, count, and blank values for each numerical field
  • Identified and removed records with blank responses to ensure data integrity
  • Eliminated duplicate records across all fields to prevent skewed results
  • Standardized inconsistent text fields in Department and Question columns
  • Documented data quality issues and cleaning procedures

2 Data Preparation & Transformation

  • Created a "Chart Source" tab with unique list of survey questions
  • Calculated response frequencies and proportions for each question
  • Computed average responses excluding zeros for accurate measurement
  • Converted counts into percentages based on total valid responses
  • Sorted questions by average response to highlight areas of strength and concern

3 Data Visualization & Insight Generation

  • Created 100% stacked bar charts for clear visual comparison
  • Implemented color coding: orange/red for negative responses (1-2), blue for positive responses (3-4)
  • Designed Likert scale visualizations for intuitive interpretation
  • Added data labels and optimized chart elements for readability
  • Developed department-specific analyses to identify patterns

Key Findings

Survey Question Analysis

Question Category Average Rating Key Insight
Work-Life Balance 3.4/4.0 Highest satisfaction area
Management Support 2.1/4.0 Primary area for improvement
Career Development 2.3/4.0 Significant improvement opportunity
Compensation 2.8/4.0 Moderate satisfaction
Work Environment 3.1/4.0 Generally positive

Departmental Patterns

  • Operations Department: Showed lowest satisfaction scores, particularly regarding management support
  • Administrative Staff: Highest satisfaction with work-life balance but concerns about career advancement
  • Field Operations: Strong team cohesion but dissatisfaction with resources and equipment
  • Engineering Division: Positive about professional development opportunities but desired more recognition

Critical Insights

  • Top Concern: Management support emerged as the most significant driver of dissatisfaction across multiple departments.
  • Positive Finding: Employees generally reported good work-life balance, indicating effective scheduling practices.
  • Opportunity Area: Career development programs showed room for significant improvement, particularly for long-tenured staff.

Analytical Techniques

  • Response frequency and proportion calculations
  • Comparative analysis across departments and roles
  • Visualization of Likert scale data

  • Stakeholder-focused reporting format development

Business Impact

The analysis enabled HR leadership to:

  • Develop targeted leadership training programs focusing on support and communication
  • Create clear advancement pathways and professional development opportunities
  • Implement tailored interventions for each department based on their unique challenges
  • Enhance employee recognition systems to boost morale
  • Guide data-driven decisions on where to invest in equipment and tools
Recommendation: Implement a phased approach starting with management training (quick win), followed by career development programs (medium-term), and concluding with compensation review (long-term). Focus initial efforts on the Operations Department where dissatisfaction is highest.
