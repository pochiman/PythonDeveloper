005 : Bank Customer Classification

Project Overview

This project involved building and comparing machine learning classification models to predict customer churn for the Bank of Mavenland. The analysis focused on identifying customers with the highest risk of leaving the bank, enabling targeted retention strategies to increase revenue and reduce customer attrition.

Through comprehensive model development and evaluation, the project demonstrated the practical application of classification algorithms to solve real-world business problems in the banking industry.

Business Context

The Bank of Mavenland was experiencing significant customer churn impacting revenue growth. As a newly hired Data Scientist, I was tasked with building predictive models to identify at-risk customers before they leave, allowing the product team to implement proactive retention campaigns.

Primary Objective: Develop and compare classification models that accurately predict customer churn, enabling targeted interventions to reduce attrition and increase company revenue.

Methodology & Analysis

1 Data Exploration & Profiling

  • Imported and analyzed 10,000 customer records from "Bank_Churn.csv"
  • Conducted comprehensive data quality assessment and descriptive statistics
  • Built scatterplot matrices to identify relationships between numeric variables
  • Created box plots segmented by churn status for numerical features
  • Developed bar charts showing churn percentages by categorical variables
  • Identified key patterns and correlations in customer behavior

2 Data Preparation & Feature Engineering

  • Removed non-predictive columns unsuitable for modeling
  • Engineered new features: "balance_to_income" ratio and "income_v_products"
  • Created dummy variables for categorical features (Geography, Gender)
  • Split data into training (80%) and testing (20%) sets
  • Ensured proper data formatting for machine learning algorithms

3 Logistic Regression Modeling

  • Built and trained logistic regression model on training data
  • Evaluated performance using confusion matrix and classification metrics
  • Calculated accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 scores
  • Plotted ROC curve and calculated AUC statistic
  • Analyzed precision-recall tradeoffs across different threshold values

4 Random Forest Modeling & Tuning

  • Implemented random forest classifier with default parameters
  • Performed hyperparameter tuning using cross-validation
  • Optimized model performance through parameter grid search
  • Evaluated final model on test data for accuracy and AUC
  • Analyzed feature importance to identify key churn drivers

Key Findings

  • Geography Impact: German customers had significantly higher churn rates than French or Spanish customers
  • Gender Difference: Female customers showed higher churn rates than male customers
  • Product Relationship: Customers with fewer products were more likely to churn
  • Credit Score: Customers with lower credit scores had higher churn rates
  • Age Factor: Middle-aged customers (40-60) showed the highest churn rates

Feature Importance Analysis

The random forest model identified the following as most important predictors of churn:

  • Age: Older customers showed higher churn rates
  • Balance: Customers with specific balance ranges were more likely to leave
  • Geography (Germany): Strong regional effect on churn behavior
  • NumOfProducts: Product engagement inversely related to churn
  • IsActiveMember: Inactive members had higher churn probability

Business Impact

The classification models enabled the Bank of Mavenland to:

  • Identify high-risk customer segments for proactive intervention.
  • Optimize retention resources by focusing on customers most likely to churn.
  • Come up with strategies based on insights from product engagement impact on loyalty.
  • Develop region-specific retention strategies based on geographic churn patterns.
  • Predict customer attrition with 86% accuracy for ongoing churn prediction.
Recommendation: Implement the tuned random forest model in production to score customers monthly. Develop targeted retention campaigns for German customers, older demographics, and those with low product engagement. Use feature importance insights to improve product offerings and customer engagement strategies.
