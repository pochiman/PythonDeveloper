Project Overview

This project involved analyzing motor vehicle theft data from the New Zealand police department to identify patterns, trends, and hotspots. The analysis helped inform public safety initiatives and awareness campaigns about when, which, and where vehicles are most likely to be stolen.

The project demonstrates my expertise in temporal pattern analysis, geographic data interpretation, and deriving actionable insights from law enforcement data.

Business Context

The New Zealand police department needed to create a data-driven public service announcement to raise awareness about vehicle theft patterns. The analysis aimed to identify specific risk factors and patterns to help citizens protect their vehicles more effectively.

Primary Question: When are vehicles most likely to be stolen, which vehicles are targeted most frequently, and where do these thefts occur most often across New Zealand?

Methodology & Analysis

1 Temporal Pattern Analysis

  • Analyzed theft frequency by year, month, and day of week
  • Converted numeric day values to day names for better interpretation
  • Created visualizations showing theft patterns over time
  • Identified seasonal and weekly patterns in theft activity
-- Thefts by day of week
SELECT DAYNAME(date_stolen) AS day_name,
COUNT(*) AS theft_count
FROM stolen_vehicles
GROUP BY DAYNAME(date_stolen)
ORDER BY theft_count DESC;

2 Vehicle Characteristics Analysis

  • Identified most and least frequently stolen vehicle types
  • Calculated average age of stolen vehicles by type
  • Analyzed luxury vs standard vehicle theft patterns
  • Examined color preferences among thieves
  • Created heat maps comparing vehicle types and colors

3 Geographic & Regional Analysis

  • Mapped theft frequency by region across New Zealand
  • Correlated theft data with population and density statistics
  • Compared theft patterns in high-density vs low-density regions
  • Created scatter plots visualizing population density vs theft rates
  • Developed regional heat maps showing theft hotspots
-- Thefts by region with population data
SELECT r.region, r.population, r.density,
COUNT(sv.vehicle_id) AS theft_count
FROM regions r
JOIN stolen_vehicles sv ON r.region = sv.region
GROUP BY r.region, r.population, r.density
ORDER BY theft_count DESC;

Key Theft Pattern Indicators

  • Temporal Patterns: Fridays showed the highest theft rates, with weekends generally having more thefts than weekdays
  • Vehicle Preferences: Sedans and station wagons were most frequently targeted, with older vehicles being more vulnerable
  • Color Analysis: Silver and white vehicles were stolen most frequently, representing over 35% of all thefts
  • Regional Hotspots: Auckland had the highest number of thefts, followed by Canterbury and Wellington
  • Density Correlation: Higher population density correlated with increased theft frequency, but not necessarily higher rates per capita
  • Vehicle Age: The average stolen vehicle was 12 years old, with certain vehicle types showing different age patterns

-- Average vehicle age by type
SELECT vehicle_type, AVG(YEAR(date_stolen) - model_year) AS avg_age
FROM stolen_vehicles
GROUP BY vehicle_type
ORDER BY avg_age DESC;

Business Impact

The analysis provided the New Zealand police department with actionable insights to:

  • Develop targeted public awareness campaigns for high-risk days and areas
  • Focus patrol resources in theft hotspots during peak times
  • Create specific advisories for owners of high-risk vehicle types
  • Implement community watch programs in high-theft regions
  • Develop prevention strategies tailored to different vehicle categories
  • Inform policy decisions regarding vehicle security requirements
Recommendation: Focus public awareness campaigns on Friday theft prevention, target owners of older sedans and station wagons in urban areas, and increase police visibility in Auckland, Canterbury, and Wellington during high-risk periods. Implement vehicle security initiatives specifically designed for the most vulnerable vehicle types and colors.
