009 : World Economic Report

Comprehensive analysis of global economic trends using World Bank and UN data to create an infographic-style report on economic development

View on GitHub
Python Pandas Seaborn Matplotlib Jupyter

Bubble chart analyzing the correlation between national wealth (GDP per capita) and population health (life expectancy)

A comprehensive infographic synthesizing key findings on GDP growth, population surge, HDI comparisons, and the critical link between electricity consumption and economic development

Project Overview

This project involved analyzing global economic indicators from the World Bank and United Nations to create a comprehensive report on worldwide economic trends from 1960-2021. The analysis included GDP growth, population trends, Human Development Index (HDI) comparisons, and relationships between economic factors and quality of life indicators.

The analysis covered economic indicators from 1960-2018 and provided insights into population growth, GDP trends, human development progress, and relationships between various economic factors.

Business Context

The World Bank's annual economic report contains crucial data on global economic performance, but the raw data requires significant processing and analysis to extract meaningful insights. This project transformed complex economic datasets into accessible visualizations that help readers understand global economic trends, regional disparities, and development progress.

Primary Question: What are the major global economic trends from 1960-2021, and how have different regions progressed in terms of development, GDP growth, and quality of life indicators?

Methodology & Analysis

1 Data Preparation & Integration

  • Imported World Bank economic data covering 1960-2018 with 200+ indicators
  • Calculated population metrics using GDP and GDP per capita data
  • Integrated United Nations Human Development Index (HDI) data for 1990-2021
  • Performed comprehensive data quality assessment and profiling
  • Filtered to 2014 data and joined with HDI data using country codes
  • Handled missing values and data inconsistencies across sources

2 Data Transformation & Pivoting

  • Created GDP pivot table with years as rows and regions as columns
  • Generated population pivot table showing regional demographic trends
  • Calculated average HDI by region for comparative analysis
  • Structured data for time series analysis of economic indicators
  • Prepared datasets for regional comparison visualizations
  • Normalized data for cross-country and cross-region comparisons

3 Visualization Development

  • Created stacked area charts showing GDP growth by region over time
  • Developed population growth visualizations with regional breakdowns
  • Built bubble charts comparing life expectancy, GDP per capita, and population
  • Designed bar charts for HDI comparison across regions
  • Created scatterplots analyzing power consumption vs. GDP relationships
  • Implemented consistent color coding across all visualizations

4 Report Compilation

  • Assembled all visualizations into a cohesive single-page report
  • Added descriptive titles and contextual explanations
  • Ensured visual consistency and readability across the report
  • Highlighted key insights and trends for reader comprehension
  • Structured the report to tell a compelling data-driven story

Key Findings

Major Economic Trends Identified

Trend Insight Impact
GDP Growth Patterns East Asia & Pacific showed the most significant GDP growth since 2000 Shift in global economic power towards Asian economies
Population Dynamics Sub-Saharan Africa experienced the highest population growth rate Increasing demographic importance of African markets
HDI Progress Europe & Central Asia maintained the highest average HDI Persistent development gap between regions
Life Expectancy Correlation Strong positive correlation between GDP per capita and life expectancy Economic development directly impacts health outcomes

Regional Development Insights

  • East Asia & Pacific: Rapid economic growth with improving HDI scores, though starting from lower base
  • Europe & Central Asia: Highest HDI levels with stable but slower economic growth
  • Latin America & Caribbean: Moderate growth with significant inequality challenges
  • Middle East & North Africa: Resource-driven economies with variable development outcomes
  • Sub-Saharan Africa: Fastest population growth but slowest economic and HDI progress
  • South Asia: Significant recent economic growth with large population base

The World Economic Report provided The Numbers magazine with:

  • Comprehensive analysis of global economic trends over six decades
  • Data-driven insights into regional development disparities
  • Visualizations that made complex economic data accessible to readers
