Project Overview

This project involved analyzing global economic indicators from the World Bank and United Nations to create a comprehensive report on worldwide economic trends from 1960-2021. The analysis included GDP growth, population trends, Human Development Index (HDI) comparisons, and relationships between economic factors and quality of life indicators.

The project demonstrates my expertise in economic data analysis, multi-source data integration, and creating compelling visualizations for data journalism.

Business Context

The Numbers magazine needed to transform complex World Bank and UN economic data into an accessible, infographic-style report that would help readers understand major global economic trends and development patterns over six decades.

Primary Question: What are the major global economic trends from 1960-2021, and how have different regions progressed in terms of development, GDP growth, and quality of life indicators?

Methodology & Analysis

1 Data Preparation & Integration

  • Imported World Bank economic data covering 1960-2018 with 200+ indicators
  • Calculated population metrics using GDP and GDP per capita data
  • Integrated United Nations Human Development Index (HDI) data for 1990-2021
  • Performed comprehensive data quality assessment and profiling
  • Filtered to 2014 data and joined with HDI data using country codes
  • Handled missing values and data inconsistencies across sources

2 Data Transformation & Pivoting

  • Created GDP pivot table with years as rows and regions as columns
  • Generated population pivot table showing regional demographic trends
  • Calculated average HDI by region for comparative analysis
  • Structured data for time series analysis of economic indicators
  • Prepared datasets for regional comparison visualizations
  • Normalized data for cross-country and cross-region comparisons

3 Visualization Development

  • Created stacked area charts showing GDP growth by region over time
  • Developed population growth visualizations with regional breakdowns
  • Built bubble charts comparing life expectancy, GDP per capita, and population
  • Designed bar charts for HDI comparison across regions
  • Created scatterplots analyzing power consumption vs. GDP relationships
  • Implemented consistent color coding across all visualizations

4 Report Compilation

  • Assembled all visualizations into a cohesive single-page report
  • Added descriptive titles and contextual explanations
  • Ensured visual consistency and readability across the report
  • Highlighted key insights and trends for reader comprehension
  • Structured the report to tell a compelling data-driven story

Key Economic Indicators

  • Asian Economic Rise: East Asia & Pacific region showed the most dramatic GDP growth since 1990
  • Population Shifts: Africa experienced the highest population growth rate while other regions stabilized
  • HDI Convergence: Developing regions showed significant HDI improvement, narrowing gaps with developed nations
  • Life Expectancy Correlation: Strong positive relationship between GDP per capita and life expectancy across all regions
  • Energy Development Link: Electricity consumption strongly correlated with economic development levels
  • Regional Disparities: Significant economic and development gaps persist between regions despite overall progress

  • Data-driven insights for feature articles on economic development
