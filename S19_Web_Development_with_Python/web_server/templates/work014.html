014 : Traffic Safety Analysis

Comprehensive analysis of NYC traffic accident data to identify patterns, trends, and contributing factors for improved public safety

Heatmap visualization of accident frequency by day of week and hour of day

Analysis of vehicle collision data showing the total number of incidents and the danger rate for each contributing factor

Project Overview

This project involved analyzing New York City traffic accident data from January 2021 to April 2023 to identify patterns, trends, and contributing factors. The analysis helped the NYPD develop targeted public safety initiatives to prevent future accidents and improve road safety across the city.

The project demonstrates advanced Excel skills including data cleaning, pivot table analysis, sophisticated chart creation, and actionable insights generation for public safety initiatives.

Business Context

The New York City Police Department needed to analyze traffic accident patterns to support their Public Safety initiative. The analysis aimed to identify when, where, and why accidents occur most frequently to develop targeted prevention strategies.

Primary Question: What are the seasonal patterns, weekly trends, and contributing factors for traffic accidents in New York City, and how can this information be used to prevent future accidents?

Methodology & Analysis

1 Seasonal Pattern Analysis

  • Calculated collision counts by month and year
  • Filtered out incomplete data (April 2023)
  • Created multi-year line charts to visualize trends
  • Identified seasonal patterns and year-over-year changes
  • Applied professional formatting for clear visualization

2 Weekly Trend Visualization

  • Extracted weekday and hour from date/time columns
  • Calculated collision counts by weekday and hour
  • Created heatmap visualizations for time-based patterns
  • Applied "white-white-red" color scale to highlight danger periods
  • Identified peak accident times throughout the week

3 Contributing Factor Analysis

  • Analyzed top 10 contributing factors by collision count
  • Calculated percentage of collisions involving injuries or fatalities
  • Applied data bars for visual comparison of danger percentages
  • Compared factors for all accidents vs. fatal accidents specifically
  • Identified most dangerous streets and their contribution percentages

Key Findings

Seasonal Patterns

Pattern Type Key Insight Implication
Monthly Trends Consistent peak in accidents during summer months (June-August) Increased traffic volume and tourism during summer requires enhanced safety measures
Year-over-Year Similar patterns across 2021 and 2022, validating seasonal consistency Predictable patterns allow for proactive resource allocation
Lowest Frequency Winter months (January-February) show reduced accident rates Potential correlation with reduced outdoor activities and travel

Weekly & Daily Patterns

  • Peak Hours: Highest accident frequency during evening rush hours (4-7 PM)
  • Weekday Patterns: Fridays showed the highest accident rates among weekdays
  • Weekend Trends: Late-night hours (11 PM-2 AM) on weekends showed elevated risk
  • Commute Correlation: Clear correlation with typical work commute patterns

Contributing Factors Analysis

  • Most Common Factor: "Driver Inattention/Distraction" was the leading cause
  • High-Risk Factors: "Following Too Closely" showed high percentage of dangerous collisions
  • Fatal Accident Factors: A different pattern emerged for fatal accidents vs. overall accidents
  • Preventable Factors: Majority of top factors involved preventable driver behaviors

Technical Implementation

Technologies Used

Excel PivotTables PivotCharts Slicers

Analytical Techniques

  • Time-series analysis for seasonal pattern identification
  • Multi-dimensional analysis using pivot tables
  • Custom heatmap creation for temporal pattern visualization
  • Percentage calculations and comparative analysis
  • Data filtering and segmentation for focused insights
  • Risk prioritization based on frequency and severity metrics

Business Impact

The analysis provided the NYPD with actionable insights to:

  • Optimize police patrol schedules based on identified high-risk time periods
  • Focus on specific contributing factors during peak accident hours
  • Develop safety campaigns addressing the most common preventable factors
  • Identify high-accident locations in need of engineering interventions
  • Prepare for increased summer accident rates with proactive measures
Recommendation: Focus enforcement efforts on weekdays during afternoon rush hours, implement targeted campaigns against driver distraction, improve infrastructure at identified high-risk locations, and use these insights to measure the impact of future safety initiatives through comparative analysis.
