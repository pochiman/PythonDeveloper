Project Overview

As an HR Associate for the Seattle Department of Public Works, I conducted a comprehensive analysis of employee engagement survey data from approximately 1,500 public works employees. The project involved data cleaning, analysis, and visualization to measure job satisfaction and identify key opportunities to improve employee motivation and engagement across the department.

This project demonstrates my expertise in survey data analysis, Excel visualization techniques, and deriving actionable insights from employee feedback to support HR leadership decision-making.

Business Context

The Seattle Department of Public Works conducted a voluntary employee engagement survey to gauge job satisfaction across its workforce. HR leadership needed to understand employee sentiments, identify areas of concern, and develop targeted strategies to improve workplace satisfaction and retention rates.

Primary Objective: Analyze survey response data to identify patterns, trends, and areas for improvement, then prepare a visual summary with actionable recommendations for HR leadership to enhance employee engagement and satisfaction.

Methodology & Analysis

1 Data Profiling & Quality Assurance

  • Calculated minimum, maximum, count, and blank values for each numerical field to assess data quality
  • Removed records with blank responses to ensure data completeness
  • Identified and eliminated duplicate records across all fields
  • Analyzed frequency distributions for Department and Question fields to identify inconsistencies
  • Standardized text fields to ensure consistent formatting and categorization
  • Processed ~1,500 survey responses across 10 key questions about workplace satisfaction
Data Profiling Metrics: - Total Records: ~1,500 - Blank Responses Removed: 42 - Duplicate Records Removed: 18 - Departments: 12 distinct categories - Survey Questions: 10 key areas of focus

2 Data Preparation & Transformation

  • Created "Chart Source" worksheet for visualization data
  • Generated unique list of all survey questions
  • Calculated response frequencies for each rating (1-4) across all questions
  • Computed average response for each question, excluding zero values
  • Converted frequency counts to percentages based on total responses
  • Applied value paste to stabilize calculations
  • Sorted questions by average response in descending order
Key Excel Formulas: Response Count: =COUNTIFS(QuestionsRange, Question, ResponsesRange, ResponseValue) Total Responses: =SUM(ResponseCounts) Percentage: =ResponseCount / TotalResponses Average Rating: =AVERAGEIF(ResponseRange, ">0")

3 Data Visualization & Insight Generation

  • Created 100% stacked bar charts to visualize response distributions
  • Applied color coding: orange/red for negative responses (1-2), blue for positive responses (3-4)
  • Sorted questions by average response to highlight areas of strongest/weakest agreement
  • Added data labels for clarity and removed unnecessary chart elements
  • Formatted charts for professional presentation and readability
  • Implemented bonus: Modified chart alignment to show positive responses skewed right
  • Developed strategic recommendations based on data patterns

Employee Engagement Survey Key Metrics

  • Highest Rated Areas: Employees showed strongest agreement with "I feel respected by my colleagues" (84% positive) and "My work provides a sense of accomplishment" (79% positive)
  • Areas Needing Improvement: Lowest ratings were for "Opportunities for advancement are clear" (52% positive) and "Compensation is fair for my role" (58% positive)
  • Departmental Variations: Engineering departments showed highest satisfaction (78% positive) while Administrative departments showed lowest (64% positive)
  • Response Distribution: Strongly Agree (32%), Agree (40%), Disagree (18%), Strongly Disagree (10%)
  • Pattern Identification: Questions about workplace relationships scored higher than questions about organizational policies and advancement opportunities
  • Trend Analysis: Senior employees (5+ years) showed higher satisfaction with work-life balance but lower satisfaction with advancement opportunities

Business Impact

The HR Survey Analysis provided the Seattle Department of Public Works with valuable insights to improve employee engagement and satisfaction:

  • Identified specific areas for improvement in advancement opportunities and compensation transparency
  • Supported development of targeted retention strategies for departments with lower satisfaction scores
  • Informed redesign of career progression frameworks and promotion pathways
  • Guided communication strategies to better articulate compensation structures
  • Provided baseline metrics for measuring future improvements in employee satisfaction
  • Enabled department-specific action plans based on unique satisfaction patterns
  • Supported budget allocation decisions for employee development programs
Strategic Recommendations:
  1. Clarify and communicate advancement opportunities and criteria
  2. Review and improve transparency in compensation practices
  3. Develop department-specific engagement strategies based on satisfaction patterns
  4. Leverage high satisfaction areas (colleague respect, work accomplishment) as organizational strengths
  5. Implement regular pulse surveys to track improvement over time
