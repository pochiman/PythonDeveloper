003 : Electricity Usage Analysis

Seasonal analysis of Morocco's electricity consumption patterns to identify peak load periods and inform infrastructure investment decisions

Project Overview

This project involved analyzing Morocco's electricity consumption patterns to identify seasonal trends and peak load periods. As a Demand Analyst for Morocco's National Power Co., the analysis focused on understanding consumption patterns across different time dimensions (hourly, daily, weekly) to support infrastructure upgrade planning and prevent potential outages.

The analysis utilized time series data collected at 10-minute intervals across three geographical zones, incorporating environmental factors such as temperature, humidity, and wind speed to understand their impact on electricity demand.

Business Context

Morocco's National Power Co. was planning significant infrastructure upgrades and needed to understand electricity consumption patterns to make informed investment decisions. The company required insights into when peak demand occurs to ensure adequate capacity and prevent service disruptions during high-usage periods.

Primary Question: What seasonal patterns occur in electricity consumption at hourly, weekly, and annual levels, and how do environmental factors influence demand across different zones?

Methodology & Analysis

1 Data Preparation & Feature Engineering

  • Imported electricity consumption data with 10-minute intervals from CSV files
  • Processed datetime information and cast to appropriate data types
  • Created calculated columns including total consumption across all three zones
  • Extracted date parts (hour, day of week, month) for seasonal analysis
  • Handled missing values and ensured data quality for time series analysis

2 Time Series Visualization

  • Resampled high-frequency data into hourly increments for trend analysis
  • Filtered and analyzed specific time periods (January 2017) for detailed examination
  • Created stacked line charts to visualize consumption patterns across three zones
  • Analyzed daily and weekly consumption trends
  • Compared consumption patterns across different seasons and weather conditions

3 Seasonal Pattern Analysis

  • Built pivot tables to aggregate consumption by day of week and hour of day
  • Created heatmaps to visualize consumption patterns across temporal dimensions
  • Analyzed correlation between environmental factors and electricity demand
  • Compared consumption patterns across different geographical zones
  • Identified peak load periods and seasonal variations

4 Environmental Factor Analysis

  • Calculated correlation coefficients between temperature and consumption
  • Analyzed impact of humidity and wind speed on electricity demand
  • Compared environmental factor influence across different zones
  • Identified strongest predictors of electricity consumption
  • Developed insights for demand forecasting

Key Findings

Seasonal Consumption Patterns

Time Dimension Peak Consumption Period Lowest Consumption Period Key Insight
Daily (Hourly) Evening hours (6-9 PM) Early morning (3-5 AM) Clear residential usage pattern with evening peaks
Weekly Weekdays Weekends Commercial/industrial demand drives weekday peaks
Seasonal Summer months Spring months Air conditioning demand significantly increases summer usage

Environmental Factor Correlations

  • Temperature Impact: Highest correlation with consumption, particularly in residential zones
  • Humidity Effect: Moderate negative correlation, as higher humidity often accompanies temperature extremes
  • Wind Speed: Weakest correlation, showing minimal direct impact on consumption patterns
  • Zone Variations: Different zones showed varying sensitivity to environmental factors

Zone-Specific Patterns

  • Zone 1: Showed strongest commercial pattern with weekday peaks
  • Zone 2: Residential characteristics with evening and weekend peaks
  • Zone 3: Mixed usage with consistent demand patterns
  • Peak Load Timing: Zones reached peak consumption at slightly different times, suggesting staggered demand
Strongest Correlation: Temperature showed the strongest positive correlation with electricity consumption (r = 0.72), indicating that higher temperatures drive increased demand, likely due to air conditioning usage.

Business Impact

The analysis provided Morocco's National Power Co. with critical insights for:

  • Infrastructure Planning: Informed decisions on capacity upgrades based on peak demand patterns
  • Load Management: Developed strategies for managing demand during identified peak periods
  • Predictive Maintenance: Scheduled maintenance during low-demand periods to minimize disruption
  • Energy Forecasting: Improved demand forecasting models incorporating seasonal and environmental factors
  • Zone-Specific Strategies: Developed targeted approaches for different geographical areas
Recommendation: Focus infrastructure investments on capacity enhancements for evening peak periods (6-9 PM) and summer months. Implement demand response programs targeting temperature-sensitive consumption to reduce peak load strain on the grid.
