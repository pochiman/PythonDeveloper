010 : Restaurant Order Analysis

Comprehensive analysis of restaurant orders to identify popular menu items, customer preferences, and business insights

View on GitHub
SQL Data Analysis Business Intelligence Customer Behavior Menu Optimization Restaurant Analytics

Exploring order date ranges, volume patterns, and large orders (12+ items)

Combined table analysis showing most/least ordered items, top 5 spending orders, and category insights

Project Overview

This project involved analyzing customer order data from Taste of the World Cafe to evaluate the performance of their new menu. The analysis identified popular menu items, customer ordering patterns, and provided data-driven recommendations for menu optimization and business strategy.

The project demonstrates my expertise in SQL data analysis, business intelligence, and deriving actionable insights from transactional data.

Business Context

Taste of the World Cafe launched a new menu at the start of the year and needed to understand customer reception, identify popular items, and optimize their menu offerings based on actual order data from a full quarter of operations.

Primary Question: Which menu items are performing well versus underperforming, what do top customers prefer, and how can the restaurant optimize its menu and operations based on order patterns?

Methodology & Analysis

1 Data Exploration & Understanding

  • Examined the menu_items table structure and content
  • Analyzed the order_details table to understand the date range and order patterns
  • Identified data quality issues and inconsistencies
  • Performed initial statistical analysis of both tables
-- Count of items by category
SELECT category, COUNT(*) as item_count,
ROUND(AVG(price), 2) as avg_price
FROM menu_items
GROUP BY category
ORDER BY item_count DESC;

2 Menu Item Analysis

  • Identified least and most expensive items across all categories
  • Analyzed Italian dishes specifically as a key category
  • Calculated average prices within each menu category
  • Compared price distributions across categories

3 Order Pattern Analysis

  • Determined the date range of available order data
  • Calculated total orders and items ordered
  • Identified orders with the highest number of items
  • Analyzed orders with more than 12 items as large orders
-- Orders with most items
SELECT order_id, COUNT(item_id) as num_items
FROM order_details
GROUP BY order_id
ORDER BY num_items DESC
LIMIT 5;

4 Customer Behavior Analysis

  • Joined menu_items and order_details tables for comprehensive analysis
  • Identified least and most ordered items and their categories
  • Analyzed top spending orders and their composition
  • Examined details of highest spend orders
  • Investigated time-based ordering patterns

Key Performance Indicators

  • Most Popular Category: American dishes were ordered most frequently
  • Least Ordered Items: Edamame and House Salad were the least popular items
  • Highest Revenue Category: Asian dishes generated the highest total revenue
  • Large Orders: 125 orders contained more than 12 items
  • Price Sensitivity: Mexican category showed the best value perception
  • Time Patterns: Highest order volume occurred during dinner hours (6-9 PM)

Technical Implementation

Technologies Used

SQL Data Analysis Business Intelligence Query Optimization Data Joins Aggregate Functions

Analytical Techniques

  • Complex SQL queries with multiple joins
  • Aggregate functions for statistical analysis
  • Data filtering and conditional queries
  • Time-based pattern analysis
  • Revenue and profitability calculations
  • Customer segmentation based on ordering behavior
-- Top 5 highest spending orders
SELECT od.order_id, SUM(mi.price) as total_spent
FROM order_details od
JOIN menu_items mi ON od.item_id = mi.menu_item_id
GROUP BY od.order_id
ORDER BY total_spent DESC
LIMIT 5;

Business Impact

The analysis provided Taste of the World Cafe with actionable insights to:

  • Optimize menu offerings by focusing on popular, high-margin items
  • Identify underperforming dishes for potential removal or improvement
  • Adjust staffing levels based on order volume patterns
  • Develop targeted promotions for less popular but profitable items
  • Inform future menu development based on cuisine popularity
  • Price menu items more effectively based on value perception
Recommendation: Focus on expanding the Asian menu category which shows strong revenue performance, consider revising or promoting underperforming items like Edamame, and optimize staffing during peak dinner hours to handle higher order volumes efficiently.
